Tony Hoare

The inventor of Quicksort

    Timeline

  • 1934 - Born - 11 January, Colombo, Sri Lanka
  • 1956 - Earned a Bachelor’s degree in classics from the University of Oxford
  • 1956 - Started studying Russian during his national service in the Royal Navy
  • 1959 - Moved to Russia for graduate work in probability and theory and computer translation of human languages at Moscow State University. He devised Quicksort during this time
  • 1960 - Returned to England and joined Elliot Brothers, Ltd where he worked on a compiler for ALGOL
  • 1968 - Accepted a professorship in computer science at Queen's University of Belfast
  • 1977 - Returned to Oxford as a computer science professor
  • 1999 - He became Senior Researcher at Microsoft in Cambridge after his retirement from Oxford

    Awards and honors

  • 1980 - Turing Award
  • 1982 - Elected Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1985 - Michael Faraday Medal And Prize
  • 2000 - Kyoto Prize
  • 2000 - Knighthood for service in computer science
“There are two ways of constructing a software design: One way is to make it so simple that there are obviously no deficiencies, and the other way is to make it so complicated that there are no obvious deficiencies. The first method is far more difficult.” - Tony Hoare
